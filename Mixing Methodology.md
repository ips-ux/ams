- **Balance**: Ensure that the track has a balanced distribution of lows, mids and high frequencies.
- **Clarity**: Each element has its own space, while reducing potential clashing frequencies.
- **Consistency**: Ensure consistent volume levels throughout the track, avoiding sudden drops or peaks.
- **Flow**: Ensure the mix supports the arrangement, enhancing the track’s energy and emotional flow.

# Mixing Methodology

### **STEP 1: VOLUME**

- **GOALS**
    - **Gain Staging**: Follow our defined process for mixing individual elements in.
    - **Consistent Levels**: Use faders to balance your mix before adding processing.
    - **Metering:** Use metering tools to monitor input/output levels to make sure nothing is clipping.
    - **Volume Shaping**: Ensure your bass/kick relationship and sidechain is dialed in.

### **STEP 2: DYNAMICS**

- **Overview**
    
    *Dynamics* refers to the relationship between volume levels over time — within a single sound or across multiple sounds.
    
    - Within-sound: e.g., transient vs. tail of a snare.
    - Between-sounds: e.g., kick vs. bass, or vocal vs. instrumental.
- **GOALS**
    - **Control Dynamics**: Even out the volume levels of audio signals to ensure consistent loudness.
    - **Increase Punch**: Enhance the impact of instruments and vocals by tightening transients.
    - **Prevent Clipping**: Limit peaks to prevent distortion and clipping in the final mix.
    - **Glue the Mix**: Create a cohesive and polished sound by smoothly blending all elements together.
- **STRATEGIES**
    - **Compression**
        - Reduces dynamic range (difference between peaks and average volume).
        - **Useful for**: Controlling peaks, gluing groups, adding consistency.
        - **Key Parameters**:
            - **Threshold**: Volume level at which compression starts.
            - **Ratio**: How aggressively volume is reduced above threshold.
            - **Attack**: How quickly compression kicks in.
                - More Attack = punchier transients.
                - Less Attack = smoother compression.
            - **Release**: How quickly compressor stops acting.
                - More Release = longer tails.
                - Less Release = snappier.
            - **Knee**: Smoothens transition around the threshold.
        
        **Recommended Plugins**:
        
        - FabFilter Pro-C 2
        - Waves SSL G-Master
        - Waves Renaissance Compressor
    - **Multiband Compression**
        - Compresses frequency ranges (low, mid, high) separately.
        - **Useful for**: Balancing tonal shifts over time, warming/darkening or brightening selectively.
        
        **Recommended Plugins**:
        
        - FabFilter Pro-MB
        - Waves C6 Multiband
        - Waves SSL G-Master Buss Compressor
    - **Limiting**
        - Acts like a compressor with **infinite ratio** — prevents peaks from exceeding 0dB.
        - **Use for**: Loudness maximization, catching peaks, master buss control.
        - **Best for**: Vocals, leads, buses, clean signals.
        
        **Recommended Plugins**:
        
        - FabFilter Pro-L 2
        - iZotope Ozone Maximizer
        - Waves L2
    - **Clipping**
        - Hard limits a signal by cutting off transients — adds harmonic distortion.
        - **Use for**: Increasing loudness while keeping punch, sound shaping.
        - **Best for**: Drums, basses, aggressive sounds.
        
        **Recommended Plugins**:
        
        - Kazrog KClip
        - T-RackS Soft Clipper
        - Ableton Glue Compressor (in soft clipping mode)
    - **Limiting VS Clipping**
        
        
        | **Aspect** | **Limiting** | **Clipping** |
        | --- | --- | --- |
        | Method | Transparent volume reduction | Waveform distortion & flattening |
        | Character | Clean, smooth | Punchy, aggressive |
        | Use Case | Vocals, groups, leads | Kicks, snares, bass |
- **SIDECHAINING**
    - Uses one sound to control the volume of another.
    - **Common use**: Ducking bass when kick hits to avoid masking.
    - **Tools**: Traditional compressor sidechain input OR volume automation plugins.
    
    **Recommended Plugins**:
    
    - Kickstart 2
    - Shaperbox 3
    - LFOtool
- **PROCESS**
    - **Select Instrument**: Identify which sound or bus/group you would like to control the dynamics of.
    - **Select Type**: Decide what type of effect would be the best fit for the sound.
    - **Set Parameters**: Adjust the attack and release times on your plugin.
    - **Blend**: Balance the wet signal with the dry signal to find a cohesive balance.
- **PLUGINS**
    - **Compressor**: Control dynamic rane, glue sounds together.
        - FabFilter Pro-C 2, Waves SSL G-Master, Renaissance Compressor.
    - **Limiter**: Maximize loudness, catch peaks.
        - FabFilter Pro-L 2, iZotope Ozone Maximizer, Waves L2.
    - **Clipper**: Add harmonic distortion, increase loudness, control transients.
        - Kazrog KClip, T-RackS Soft Clipper, Ableton Glue Comp.
    

### **STEP 3: EQ**

- **GOALS**
    - **Cut Unwanted Frequencies**: Use high-pass and low-pass filters to remove unnecessary frequency information.
    - I**dentify Resonances**: Use narrow-band EQ cuts to remove resonant or harsh frequencies.
    - **Enhance Frequencies**: Use wide bands to boost frequencies which enhance the character and presence of specific sounds.
    - **Subtractive EQ First:** Apply subtrative EQ before additive EQ to remove problems first.
    - **EQ in Context:** make adjustments within the context of your full mix.
- **EQ SHAPES**
    - **Bell** - standard cut/boost over a certain frequency
    - **High pass** - remove low frequencies below cutoff
    - **Low pass** - remove high frequencies above cutoff
    - **Band Pass** - high pass + low pass
    - **Shelf** - flat boost/reduction
    - **Band Reject** - remove frequencies
- **STRATEGY**
    - **Assess**: Identify sounds or groups in your mix which may have unwanted frequencies.
    - **High-Pass Filtering**: Apply low cuts to any sounds which may be creating mud in your mix.
    - **Remove Mud**: Identify muddy frequencies (200-600Hz) and remove information from sounds in that area to create clarity.
    - **Mid-Side EQ**: Use mid-side EQ to separately balance and adjust the mono and stereo fields.
    - **Boosts**: Gently boost any frequencies using analog EQ which could benefit from enhancement.
    - **Remove Resonance**: Identify resonant frequencies in your mix to remove.
- **AUTOMATIONS**
    - Low cut entire mix for first 8-bars of your intro
    - Low cut basses in your break and build
    - High cut hats/synths/vocals during beginning of break
    - Low cut last bar of 8-bar drop phrase
    - Low cut basses in your 2nd break
    - Low cut entire mix for last 8-bars of your outro
- **PLUGINS**
    - **Parametric EQ**: Gain precise control over specific frequency bands.
        - FabFilter Pro-Q 3, Waves Q10, iZotope Neutron EQ.
    - **Analog EQ**: Add warm, character, and frequencies in a natural way.
        - Waves API 550, Waves PuigTec, Waves SSL E-Channel.
    - **Dynamic EQ**: Adjust frequency dynamically.
        - FabFilter Pro-Q 3, iZotope Neutron, Waves F6
- **MIXING FREQUENCY GUIDE**
    - **GENERAL EQ TIPS**
        - **High-Pass Filters**: Apply to most elements except kick and bass to prevent low-frequency build-up.
        - **Low-Pass Filters**: Use to remove unnecessary high frequencies from bass elements.
        - **Subtractive EQ**: Often more effective than boosting; cutting unwanted frequencies can create a cleaner mix.
        - **EQ in Context**: Always EQ with the full mix playing to understand how adjustments affect the overall sound.
        - **Referencing**: Compare your mix with professional tracks to understand frequency distribution.
    - **SUB-BASS (20-60 Hz)**
        - **Elements**: Sub-bass, low-end of kick drums
        - **Purpose**: Adds power and weight; felt more than heard.
        - **Tips**: Use a high-pass filter on other elements to avoid muddiness. Ensure clarity by cutting unwanted frequencies.
    - **BASS (60-250 Hz)**
        - **Elements**: Basslines, body of kick drums
        - **Purpose**: Provides the fundamental thump and warmth.
        - **Tips**: Balance bass and kick to avoid clashing. Use EQ to carve space (e.g., boost one, cut the other).
    - **LOW-MIDS (250-500 Hz)**
        - **Elements**: Low-end of synths, lower harmonics of instruments
        - **Purpose**: Adds fullness and body.
        - **Tips**: Watch for muddiness; too much can make the mix sound boxy. Subtractive EQ can help clean this range.
    - **MIDS (500-2000 Hz)**
        - **Elements**: Mid-range synths, guitars, vocals
        - **Purpose**: Essential for the presence and clarity of melodic elements.
        - **Tips**: Balance is key. Too much can sound harsh; too little can make the mix hollow.
    - **HIGH-MIDS (2-5 kHz)**
        - **Elements**: Snare drums, claps, leads, vocals
        - **Purpose**: Adds clarity and attack; helps elements cut through the mix.
        - **Tips**: Be careful with boosting; this range can quickly become fatiguing.
    - **PRESENCE (5-10 kHz)**
        - **Elements**: Hi-hats, cymbals, synth sparkle
        - **Purpose**: Adds sheen and detail.
        - **Tips**: Enhances the crispness of percussive elements. Avoid excessive boosting to prevent harshness.
    - **AIR (10-20 kHz)**
        - **Elements**: High-end of cymbals, atmospheric effects
        - **Purpose**: Adds a sense of space and airiness.
        - **Tips**: Use sparingly to add polish. Excessive boosting can introduce noise.
- **PROBLEM SOLVING**
    - **Resonances**
        - Resonances are when a sound is highly concentrated in a specific frequency
        - Can sound like a whistling/humming/piercing/shrill sound
        - If a sound feels harsh or muddy to the ear, check a spectrum analyzer to see if the frequencies are “spikey” in a certain area
        - Also, you can sweep through frequencies with an EQ or filter and identify which frequency ranges are causing the problem
        - Use EQ filters to counter-balance these spikey frequencies, adjusting them until they seem more “flat”
    - **Masking**
        - Be careful when having multiple instruments playing too heavily in the same range; clashing frequencies
        - If two sounds are playing that have similar frequencies, try opposing EQ moves; reduce frequencies in one sound where another is concentrated
        - During sound selection, aim to find opposing sounds from the beginning to avoid difficult challenges in the mixing process.
    - **Low Frequencies**
        - Low frequencies clash easily → normal speaks and even subwoofers have a difficult time processing multiple low-frequency sounds at once
        - General practice: don’t allow two instruments to play sub frequencies at the same time
        - Avoid low-end clashing: High-pass filter most elements that don’t need low-end and use Sidechain to duck our main elements when kick/snare play to

### **STEP 4: IMAGING**

- **GOALS**
    - **Widen Soundstage**: Create a broader and more immersive listening experience by expanding the stereo field.
    - **Enhance Separation**: Improve the clarity and distinction between different elements in the mix.
    - **Create Depth**: Add a sense of depth and spatial dimension to the audio.
    - **Focus Elements**: Position sounds precisely within the stereo field to achieve a balanced and cohesive mix.
- **IMAGING TYPES**
    - **Stereo Widening:** Use stereo plugins to increase presence and width of specific elements.
    - **Mid/Side Processing**: Apply mid/side EQ to enhance stereo width without affecting center.
    - **Panning**: Use panning to position elements across stereo field, creating space and separation.
    - **Avoid Phase Issues**: Check mono compatibility of key sounds to mitigate phase cancellation.
    - **Layering**: Create width using layering and panning of complementary elements.
- **STRATEGY**
    - **Analyze**: Assess the stereo field of your mix to see where there could be additional width or panning.
    - **Panning**: Begin by individually panning sounds in the mix to create stereo variation.
    - **Widening**: Apply imaging tools to begin widening those sounds which could use additional width.
    - **EQ**: Use mid-side EQ to remove low stereo frequencies to maintain a clean mix.
    - **Compatibility**: Verify your sounds and mix as a whole sound cohesive in both mono and stereo.
    - **Automate**: Create variation in width or panning for specific sounds in your mix.
- **PLUGINS**
    - **Stereo Enhancer**: Expand or narrow the stereo width of a sound.
        - iZotope Ozone Imager, Waves S1 Imager.
    - **Chorus**: Create a more natural, wide, multi-voice effect.
        - TAL Chorus-LX, Soundtoys Crystalizer, Waves Doubler
    - **Panning**: Place sounds on either side of the stereo field.
        - Soundtoys PanMan, Waves S1 Imager, Ableton Auto Pan.
    

### **STEP 5: HARMONICS**

- **GOALS**
    - **Enhance Warmth**: Add analog warmth and character to digital recordings.
    - **Increase Harmonics**: Enrich the sound with additional harmonics for a fuller tone.
    - **Improve Presence**: Make elements stand out more clearly in the mix.
    - **Add Punch**: Enhance the impact and punch of drums and other rhythmic elements.
- **STRATEGY**
    - **Select Instrument**: Identify which sound you would like to add compression or limiting to.
    - **Select Type**: Decide whether saturation, distortion, or other type would be most impactful.
    - **Apply**: Begin with subtle setting to avoid over-processing or adding harshness.
    - **Blend**: Balance the wet signal with the dry signal to find a cohesive balance.
    - **Multiband**: Consider targeting specific frequencies of a sound which could benefit from added harmonics.
    - **Automate**: Try varying the dry/wet over time to create variation and interest in the sound.
- **PLUGINS**
    - **Saturator:** Add harmonic warmth and richness.
        - FabFilter Saturn, Soundtoys Decapitator, Waves J37 Tape.
    - **Distortion**: Add grit and intensity.
        - Izotope Trash 2, Output Thermal, SoundToys Devil-Loc.
    - **Exciter**: Enhance clarity and presence
        - Izotope Ozone Exciter, Waves Aphex Aural Exciter.
    - **Amp**: Simulate amplifier sound.
        - NI Guitar Rig 6, Waves GTR 3, Blue Cat Audio Free Amp.
    - **Overdrive**: Add smooth, warm distortion tonality.
        - SoundToys Radiator, NI Driver, TSE Audio R47.
    - **Fuzz**: Create fuzziness and high end.
        - The Fuzz, iZotope Thermal, Audio Damage FuzzPlus 3.
    

### **STEP 6: SPACE**

- **GOALS**
    - **Add Space**: Create a sense of depth and spatial dimension in the mix.
    - **Enhance Atmosphere**: Enrich the overall mood and ambiance of a track.
    - **Smooth Transitions**: Use subtle reverb and delay to soften and blend transitions between sounds.
    - **Create Texture**: Add complexity and interest by layering different reverb and delay effects.
- **STRATEGY**
    - **Select Instrument**: Identify which sound you would like to add spacial effects to.
    - **Select Type**: Decide whether reverb or delay would be the best fit for the sound.
    - **Set Parameters**: Adjust the decay and pre-delay, experiment with different types.
    - Blend: Balance the wet signal with the dry signal to avoid overpowering the mix.
    - **EQ**: Roll off unnecessary frequencies to reduce potential muddiness.
    - **Automate**: Create automations for the reverb and delay levels if applicable.
- **REVERB TYPES**
    - **Hall**: Simulate large, open spaces using spacious, lush reverbs.
        - ValhallaRoom, Waves H-Reverb.
    - **Chamber**: Simulate medium reflective spaces by creating a smooth, dense reverb.
        - Altiverb, Waves Abbey Road Chamber.
    - **Room**: Simulate small to medium rooms by adding natural, intimate reverb.
        - ValhallaRoom, FabFilter Pro-R.
    - **Place**: Simulate metal plate reverbs by creating a bright, smooth reverb.
        - Soundtoys Little Plate, Waves AR Plates.
    - **Spring**: Simulate spring reverb by adding a distinctive, boingy reverb.
        - PSP SpringBox, Softube Spring Reverb
    
- **DELAY TYPES**
    - **Analog**: Add warm and character to a sound with gradual decay.
        - Soundtoys Echoboy, Arturia Delay.
    - **Digital**: Create clear, accurate echos using clean, precise repeats.
        - FabFilter Timeless 3, Waves H-Delay.
    - **Tape**: Vintage sound and character with warm, saturated artifacts.
        - Waves J37, u-he Satin.
    - **Ping-Pong**: Clean, wide stereo effects with alternation between left and right.
        - Soundtoys EchoBoy, Ableton Ping Pong.
    - **Slapback**: Add depth to vocals with single, short repeats.
        - Waves ADT, UAD EP-34 Tape Echo.
    - **FX Types**
        - **Automation**: These tools are great at creating tension on individual instruments or master channel over the course of a build or breakdown.
            - Endless Smile, OneKnob, Toolroom Infinite.
    

### **STEP 7: INSPECT**

- **GOALS**
    - **Frequency Balance**: Ensure that all frequency ranges are well-represented and balanced, identifying any problematic peaks or dips that need adjustment.
    - **Dynamic Range**: Monitor the dynamic range to ensure that the mix maintains clarity and punch without excessive compression or limiting.
    - **Stereo Imaging**: Assess the stereo field to confirm that elements are appropriately panned and the mix has a cohesive and engaging spatial image.
    - **Phase and Clarity**: Check for phase issues and overall mix clarity to avoid phase cancellation and ensure that all elements are distinctly audible.
- **STRATEGY**
    - **Frequency Balance**: Use a spectrum analyzer to identify any areas lacking or sticking out in the mix. Compare to reference tracks.
    - **Dynamic Range**: Use a loudness meter to check overall loudness of your mix.
    - **Stereo Image**: Verify overall stereo distribution of your mix, as well as mono compatibility.
    - **Phase**: Check for phase issues between stereo channels.
- **PLUGINS**
    - **Spectrum Analyzers**: Visualize your frequency spectrum.
        - Voxengo SPAN, iZotope Insight.
    - **Loudness Meters**: Measure loudness and amplitude of audio.
        - Youlean Loudness, Waves WLM, iZotope Insight / Tonal Balance.
    - **Stereo Analyzers**: Visualize stereo width and spacial distribution.
        - iZotope Insight, Flux Stereo Tool, Brainworx bx_control.
    

# Mixing Checklist

### VOLUME

- [ ]  All lead elements are distinct and clear.
- [ ]  There is no audible unnecessary distortion on key elements.
- [ ]  Levels of key elements sound consistent with reference tracks.
- [ ]  Levels of each section are consistent with reference tracks.
- [ ]  Your kick/bass sidechain dialed in appropriately.
- [ ]  There ample headroom available for mastering process.
- [ ]  Overall volume is consistent through the track.
- [ ]  Your build up isn’t too quiet, or too loud for your drop.
- [ ]  Your drop isn’t abrasively loud or underwhelming.

### DYNAMICS

- [ ]  Dynamics of all groups are fully controlled.
- [ ]  All potential peaks and resonant dynamics have been controlled.
- [ ]  Sounds in each group are glued together dynamically.
- [ ]  Groups aren’t clipping at any point in your song.
- [ ]  Mix doesn’t sound too flat or compressed.
- [ ]  Drums have adequate punch and impact.
- [ ]  Unnecessary audible distortion isn’t heard in any primary sounds.

### EQ

- [ ]  Low frequencies are removed from elements competing with kick/bass.
- [ ]  Kick and bass are clean and clear.
- [ ]  Unnecessary frequencies, especially low to mid frequencies, have been removed from all instruments and groups.
- [ ]  Primary sounds are not clashing with the frequencies of any supporting sounds.
- [ ]  All resonant frequencies have been identified and removed.
- [ ]  Low end frequencies reduced in breaks and builds to create contrast with your drop.
- [ ]  Low end sounds have been removed from the beginning of your intro and end of your outro.

### IMAGING

- [ ]  Full stereo field is utilized throughout song.
- [ ]  Rhythmic elements and one shots are panned appropriately.
- [ ]  Drop features the greatest stereo width utilization.
- [ ]  Stereo width contrast exists between your break/build and your drop.
- [ ]  Frequencies below 150-200 Hz have been placed in mono.
- [ ]  All potential phase issues have been addressed.
- [ ]  Mix is fully compatible in mono.

### SPACE

- [ ]  Mix utilizes all areas of the 3-dimensional space.
- [ ]  Transitions are used to blend sections.
- [ ]  Both reverb and delay been used tastefully throughout your mix.
- [ ]  Mix sounds layered and immersive.
- [ ]  Similar sounds have similar spatial processing, putting them in the same 3D space.
- [ ]  All long reverb or delay tails have been managed.

### HARMONICS

- [ ]  Harmonics been added to all applicable instruments.
- [ ]  Primary sounds are all fully present in the mix.
- [ ]  No instruments sound overly distorted.

### INSPECT

- [ ]  Frequency spectrum is balanced appropriately.
- [ ]  Frequency distribution matches that of your reference tracks.
- [ ]  Dynamic range between 8-12 dB.
- [ ]  RMS is between -18 to -14 dBFS.
- [ ]  Peak levels do not exceed -6 dBFS.
- [ ]  Integrated LUFS level is between -14 to -16 LUFS.
- [ ]  Full stereo spectrum is effectively utilized.
- [ ]  All phasing issues have been corrected.
- [ ]  Mix sounds consistent across a variety of systems.
- [ ]  All unwanted clipping, distortion, and artifacts have been removed.
