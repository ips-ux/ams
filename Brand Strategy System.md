# Brand Strategy System

## 0. System Overview

![Screenshot 2025-05-07 at 9.21.26 PM.png](attachment:48e20e88-7843-4970-95de-c3281929a243:Screenshot_2025-05-07_at_9.21.26_PM.png)

- **PERSONA**
    
    *Overall persona of your brand.*
    
    - **Components**
        - **Core Identity**: Values and beliefs which define you as an artist.
        - **Personality**: Key personality traits and characteristics.
        - **Personal Story**: Background, experiences, and inspirations that shaped you as an artist.
        - **Unique Qualities**: Distinctive skills and talents which differentiate you from other artists.
        - **Emotional Appeal**: The specific emotions you will elicit in fans through your art.
        - **Demographics**: Your basic demographic information such as gender, age, location, ect.
- **ARCHETYPE**
    
    *Character-driven brand identity.*
    
    - **Archetype Wheel**
        
        ![Untitled](https://prod-files-secure.s3.us-west-2.amazonaws.com/bdb6ec4d-599f-4ea4-becd-d171452fb51a/dc0d8f84-1aa0-4736-97cd-a295e820f456/Untitled.png)
        
    - **Components**
        - **Archetype**: The specific archetype which represents you as an artist.
        - **Emotional Resonance**: The emotions and messages which resonate with your music and persona.
        - **Integration**: How the archetype presents itself through your art.
        - **Visual Elements**: Visual representations and symbols of the archetype.
        - **Audience Connection**: How the archetype helps you connect with the audience.
    - **Archetype Descriptions**
        - **The Innocent**
            
            The Innocent archetype embodies purity, optimism, and a yearning for happiness and freedom. Musicians who align with this archetype often produce music that is uplifting and filled with positive messages, aiming to inspire and bring joy to their audience. 
            
            Their songs frequently explore themes of love, hope, and the simple pleasures of life. They strive to maintain a clean and optimistic image, avoiding controversy and negativity. Their greatest fear is being punished or criticized for doing something wrong, which drives their efforts to remain virtuous and sincere. Artists like Taylor Swift, whose wholesome and relatable persona resonates with many, Alessia Cara, known for her positive and empowering songs, and Jason Mraz, whose music radiates love and positivity, exemplify the Innocent archetype.
            
            - **Core Desire**: To be free and happy.
            - **Goal**: To experience paradise.
            - **Fear**: Being punished for doing something wrong.
            - **Strategy**: Do things right, be optimistic.
            - **Examples**
                - **Odesza**: Known for their uplifting, positive sound and creating feel-good music experiences.
                - **Louis The Child**: Produces bright, cheerful music that aims to bring happiness and positivity to listeners.
                - **Paul McCartney**: Celebrated for his optimistic and joyful music, often reflecting themes of love and peace.
                - **Other Examples**: Taylor Swift, Alessia Cara, Jason Mraz.
        - **The Everyman**
            
            The Everyman archetype is centered around belonging and connecting with others. These musicians are often seen as relatable and approachable, making their fans feel understood and included. Their music speaks to common experiences and emotions, creating a sense of community and shared understanding. 
            
            They aim to be down-to-earth and fear being excluded or standing out too much. By developing solid virtues and remaining humble, they cultivate a loyal fan base. Ed Sheeran is a notable example with his relatable lyrics and unpretentious image. Other examples include Khalid, known for his accessible and emotive music, and John Mayer, who blends introspection with an everyman appeal.
            
            - **Core Desire**: To belong, connect with others.
            - **Goal**: To be part of a community.
            - **Fear**: Being left out or standing out too much.
            - **Strategy**: Develop ordinary solid virtues, be down-to-earth.
            - **Examples**
                - **Zedd**: Presents himself as an approachable and friendly figure in the EDM scene, producing widely accessible and enjoyable music.
                - **Porter Robinson**: Known for his genuine connection with fans and relatable themes in his music, like personal growth and nostalgia.
                - **Other Examples**: Ed Sheeran, Khalid, John Mayer.
        - **The Hero**
            
            The Hero archetype is defined by the desire to prove one's worth through courageous acts and mastery. Musicians with this archetype often aim to inspire and empower their audience, showcasing strength, resilience, and determination in their work. 
            
            Their music frequently addresses overcoming challenges and achieving greatness. They fear weakness and vulnerability, pushing themselves to be as strong and competent as possible. Artists like Beyoncé, known for her powerful presence and inspirational music, Kendrick Lamar, whose lyrics often highlight societal issues and personal struggles, and Bruce Springsteen, who sings about the struggles and victories of everyday life, are quintessential Heroes in the music industry.
            
            - **Core Desire**: To prove one's worth through courageous acts.
            - **Goal**: Expert mastery in a way that improves the world.
            - **Fear**: Weakness, vulnerability.
            - **Strategy**: Be as strong and competent as possible.
            - **Examples**
                - **Calvin Harris**: Known for his rise from obscurity to become one of the biggest names in electronic dance music, consistently delivering hit songs.
                - **Martin Garrix**: Young superstar who rapidly ascended the ranks of the EDM world through talent and hard work.
                - **Beyonce**: Celebrated for her powerful performances, work ethic, and messages of empowerment and resilience.
                - **Other Examples**: Kendrick Lamar, Bruce Springsteen.
        - **The Outlaw / Rebel**
            
            Outlaws reject societal norms and live by their own rules. They are bold, daring, and often controversial, challenging conventional boundaries.
            
            Musicians who embody this archetype often create provocative and disruptive music that seeks to bring about change. They thrive on being unconventional and are not afraid to shock or disrupt. Their greatest fear is being powerless or ineffectual, and they aim to be a force for revolution. 
            
            - **Core Desire**: Revenge or revolution.
            - **Goal**: To overturn what isn’t working.
            - **Fear**: Being powerless or ineffectual.
            - **Strategy**: Disrupt, destroy, or shock.
            - **Examples**
                - **Malaa**: Known for his “illegal” persona, going against traditional norms and ideals through his music and brand.
                - **Gesaffelstein**: Known for his dark, rebellious sound and aesthetic, often pushing the boundaries of techno and electronic music.
                - **Madonna**: Constantly reinvented herself, challenged societal norms, and pushed the boundaries of pop music and culture.
        - **The Creator**
            
            The Creator archetype is focused on innovation and the creation of things of enduring value. Musicians who embody this archetype are highly creative and driven by a desire to realize their vision. 
            
            They aim to produce original and lasting work, often pushing the boundaries of their art. Their fear is mediocrity, and they strive for artistic control and excellence. Prince, known for his prolific and innovative output, is an example of the Creator archetype.
            
            - **Core Desire**: To create things of enduring value.
            - **Goal**: To realize a vision.
            - **Fear**: Mediocrity.
            - **Strategy**: Develop artistic control and skill.
            - **Examples:**
                - **Skrillex**: A groundbreaking figure in dubstep who continually pushes the limits of electronic music production.
                - **Flume**: Known for his innovative production style and ability to create fresh, unique sounds in electronic music.
                - **David Bowie**: Known for his creativity, innovation, and ability to reinvent himself and his music over decades.
                - **Other Examples:** Prince, Frank Ocean, Lorde.
        - **The Lover**
            
            The Lover archetype is driven by a desire for intimacy and deep emotional connections. Musicians who embody this archetype create passionate and heartfelt music that often explores themes of love, romance, and relationships. 
            
            They seek to connect deeply with their audience and fear being alone or unloved. Their strategy is to become more physically and emotionally attractive, drawing people in with their charisma and emotional depth. 
            
            - **Core Desire**: Intimacy and experience.
            - **Goal**: Being in a relationship with the people, work, and surroundings they love.
            - **Fear**: Being alone, a wallflower, unwanted, unloved.
            - **Strategy**: Become more physically and emotionally attractive.
            - **Examples**
                - **Kygo**: Pioneered the tropical house genre, creating music that evokes feelings of warmth and passion.
                - **Illenium**: Known for his highly emotional music that often centers around themes of love and heartbreak.
                - **Adele**: Celebrated for her deeply emotional songs about love, heartbreak, and personal relationships.
        - **The Jester**
            
            Jesters bring joy and fun, often using humor and playfulness to entertain and connect with their audience. They don’t take themselves too seriously and enjoy creating a light-hearted atmosphere. The Jester archetype is all about living in the moment and having a great time. 
            
            Musicians who embody this archetype create fun, playful, and entertaining music that seeks to lighten up the world and bring joy to their audience. They fear boredom and strive to be humorous and engaging. Their strategy is to play, make jokes, and be funny, creating a light-hearted and enjoyable experience for their fans.
            
            - **Core Desire**: To live in the moment with full enjoyment.
            - **Goal**: To have a great time and lighten up the world.
            - **Fear**: Being bored or boring others.
            - **Strategy**: Play, make jokes, be funny.
            - **Examples**
                - **Fisher**: Known for his humorous and playful persona, both in his music and social media presence.
                - **Dillon Francis**: Known for his humorous and playful persona, both in his music and social media presence.
                - **Lil Nas X**: Recognized for his fun, upbeat music and energetic, entertaining performances.
        - **The Sage**
            
            The Sage archetype seeks truth and understanding through wisdom and analysis. Musicians who embody this archetype often produce thoughtful and reflective music, seeking to impart knowledge and provoke deep thought in their listeners. 
            
            They aim to understand the world and fear being misled or ignorant. Their strategy is to seek out information and knowledge, often through self-reflection and deep analysis. 
            
            - **Four Tet**: Renowned for his intelligent, genre-blending music that often explores intricate rhythms and textures.
            - **Brian Eno**: Known for his ambient music and intellectual approach to sound and music production.
            - **Bob Dylan**: Celebrated for his insightful lyrics and contributions to music and culture, often addressing profound themes and social issues.
        - **The Magician**
            
            The Magician archetype is characterized by the desire to understand and influence the fundamental laws of the universe. 
            
            Musicians who embody this archetype create transformative and often mystical music that seeks to make dreams come true and bring about change. They fear unintended negative consequences and strive to develop a vision and live by it.
            
            - **Core Desire**: Knowledge of the fundamental laws of how the world or universe works.
            - **Goal**: To make dreams come true.
            - **Fear**: Unintended negative consequences.
            - **Strategy**: Develop a vision and live by it.
            - **Examples**
                - **Deadmau5**: Known for his innovative music production and visually stunning live performances that create a sense of magic.
                - **Rezz**: Known for her hypnotic branding and live performances, as well as magic-influenced art.
                - **Other Examples**: Travis Scott, FKA twigs, David Bowie.
        - **The Caregiver**
            
            The Caregiver archetype is all about nurturing and helping others. Musicians who embody this archetype create music that comforts, supports, and uplifts their listeners. 
            
            They often address themes of compassion, love, and selflessness. Their goal is to protect and care for others, and they fear selfishness and ingratitude. By doing things for others and providing a sense of comfort, they build a strong, supportive connection with their audience. 
            
            - **Core Desire**: To protect and care for others.
            - **Goal**: To help others.
            - **Fear**: Selfishness and ingratitude.
            - **Strategy**: Do things for others, service.
            - **Examples**
                - **Above & Beyond**: Known for their uplifting, emotionally resonant music and a strong sense of community among their fans.
                - **Rüfüs Du Sol**: Creates music that provides emotional comfort and connection, fostering a sense of togetherness.
                - **Taylor Swift**: Celebrated for her heartfelt songs and her supportive, nurturing relationship with her fans.
        - **The Ruler**
            
            The Ruler archetype desires control and order, aiming to create a prosperous and successful community or family. 
            
            Musicians who embody this archetype often exert a significant influence over their careers and the music industry, seeking to maintain stability and control. They fear chaos and being overthrown, and their strategy is to exercise power and leadership. 
            
            - **Core Desire**: Control.
            - **Goal**: Create a prosperous, successful family or community.
            - **Fear**: Chaos, being overthrown.
            - **Strategy**: Exercise power.
            - **Examples**
                - **Eric Prydz**: Known for his meticulous production standards and leading the progressive house genre.
                - **Tiesto**: A dominant force in the EDM world, known for his ability to consistently deliver and shape the direction of dance music.
                - **Jay-Z**: Recognized for his dominant presence in the music industry and his influence as a business mogul.
        - **The Explorer**
            
            The Explorer archetype seeks freedom and the discovery of new experiences. Musicians who align with this archetype are known for their adventurous spirit and willingness to push boundaries. 
            
            Their music often explores themes of self-discovery, freedom, and the journey to find a better, more authentic life. They fear being trapped or conforming to societal norms, and they continuously seek out new experiences to avoid inner emptiness. 
            
            - **Core Desire**: The freedom to find out who you are through exploring the world.
            - **Goal**: To experience a better, more authentic life.
            - **Fear**: Getting trapped, conformity, inner emptiness.
            - **Strategy**: Journey, seek out and experience new things.
            - **Examples**:
                - **Bonobo**: Known for his genre-blending music and exploring diverse sounds and influences.
                - **Grimes**: An artist who constantly explores new sonic territories and visual aesthetics.
                - **Billie Eilish**: She’s known for her distinctive style and boundary-pushing music and branding.
                - **M.I.A.**: Celebrated for her eclectic music and exploration of different cultural influences.
- **AESTHETIC**
    
    *Look and feel of your brand.*
    
    - **Components**
        - **Visual Style**: Color schemes and imagery which define your look.
        - **Appearance**: Your look, style, and vibe as an artist.
        - **Brand Materials**: The look and feel of you logo, album artwork, show flyers, ect.
        - **Social Content**: The aesthetic of your content, press pictures, videos, ect.
        - **Performance**: The live visuals you use in shows and in visualizers.

## 6. AI Brand Prompts

### **I. How to Use This Template**

1. **Fill out Brand Description completely** to give the AI a detailed understanding of your brand.
2. **Copy and paste the completed template,** followed by the Brand Strategy Questions below, when asking specific questions or requesting outputs.
3. **Update it regularly** as your brand evolves or goals shift.
4. We recommend [**ChatGPT**](https://chatgpt.com/) as the AI tool to use for these exercises.

### II. Brand Description

*Complete the questions below to provide the AI a detailed overview of your brand. Please be as detailed as possible here, as these quality of your responses below will directly correlate the the quality of the outputs in the following section.*

- **Artist Name**: My stage name is _______.
- **Genre/Style**: I create _______ (dark house, hypnotic techno, uplifting trance) music.
- **Key Influences**: My sound is inspired by _______ (artists, genres, movements).
- **Description of Your Sound**: My music is _______ (e.g., dark, uplifting, hypnotic) and features _______ (e.g., driving basslines, cinematic soundscapes).
- **Brand Overview**: I’m creating a brand which I want to be known for being ________ (e.g. dark, hypnotic, uplifting, funny, luxurious).
- **Brand Story**: My story is about _______ (e.g., transformation, rebellion, perseverance).

### III. Brand Strategy Prompts

*We recommend pasting your Brand Description above, and paste these questions below to ensure the AI has a detailed overview of your brand from which it will generate these answers.*

### **1. Brand Persona**

- "Based on this Brand Description above, what unique Brand Promise can I create to stand out as an artist?"
- "How can I communicate my story and vibe effectively in my visuals and messaging?"
- “What elements from my story as an artist could I include to tell this story effectively?”
- “What key words should I use to describe my brand?”

### **2. Brand Archetype**

- “Given the Brand Description above, what brand archetype would most resonate with my project and why?”
- "How can I use symbolism and emotional appeal to differentiate myself from other artists in my genre?"
- "Suggest ways I can visually and emotionally express my archetype in my branding."
- "How can I refine my Brand Archetype [insert archetype] to attract more fans?”

### **3. Brand Aesthetic**

- "What color palettes, typography, and design elements would align with my brand, given the Brand Description above?”
- "What are some psychological associations with [specific colors], and how can I use them in my visuals?”
- "Help me refine my visual identity to better reflect my sound and brand promise, given the Brand Description above.”
- “What kind of visual appearance and clothing could I incorporate into my performances to reinforce this branding?”
- "What visual themes should I use to align with my brand’s [specific vibe] aesthetic?”

### IV. Additional Prompts

- "Suggest song titles based on my Brand Description above, along with suggested lyrics that would align with that title.”
- "Suggest logo concepts and symbolism that reflect my archetype and aesthetic.”
- “What are examples of press or marketing stunts I could utilize to build excitement around my brand”
- “Please create an Artist Bio for me based on the Brand Description above. Along with specifically formatted and shortened bios for Instagram, Spotify, and other relevant social and streaming platforms.”
- "How can I make my artist brand stand out in the [specific genre] scene while staying authentic?”
- "Suggest three ways to communicate my brand story visually through my logo, social media, and stage design.”
- "What types of stage visuals or props would complement my [specific genre and archetype] brand?”
- "How can I align my promotional strategy with my [insert archetype] Brand Persona?”
- "How can I showcase my Brand Promise in creative content across platforms?”
- "What kind of interludes or intros would enhance my brand’s storytelling in an live performance setting?”

## Branding Checklist

## Design

- **Artwork**
    - [ ]  Features strong use of your primary color(s).
    - [ ]  Consistent fonts which align with your brand.
    - [ ]  Song title featured with aligns with your brand.
    - [ ]  Unique and stands out.
    - [ ]  Artist name and song title are readable on a thumbnail size.
    - [ ]  Feature your artist logo in the cover art.
    - [ ]  .JPEG format with minimum size of 3000 x 3000 pixels.
    - [ ]  Layered PSD file included as well.
- **Biography**
    - [ ]  Includes overarching vision for your project.
    - [ ]  Includes notable labels released on.
    - [ ]  Includes notable artist DJ support.
    - [ ]  Includes notable festivals, clubs, and events played.
- **Logo**
    - [ ]  You have logo in .png, .jpeg, and .vector formats.
    - [ ]  Your logo have transparent backgrounds.
    - [ ]  Your .png and .jpeg logos are a minimum of 1000 pixels on height and width.
    - [ ]  You logo has been tested to fit on festival or show flyers.
    - [ ]  Your logo has been tested to fit on various merch pieces.
- **Photos**
    - [ ]  Your press pictures feature elements matching your primary brand colors.
    - [ ]  Press picture clearly shows your face.
    - [ ]  Picture is unique, captivating, and interesting.
    - [ ]  Showcases your style and aesthetic as an artist.
    - [ ]  Pictures are minimum of 2000 pixels width or height.
- **Press Kit**
    - [ ]  Contains at least 3 high quality press pictures.
    - [ ]  Contains at least one high quality performance picture.
    - [ ]  Brand book is included.
    - [ ]  Artist biography is included.
    - [ ]  Contains logo in .png and .vector formats.

## Color Theory

*Color theory plays a critical role in branding because colors evoke emotions, create associations, and influence fan behavior. Understanding how to use color strategically can enhance brand identity, improve brand recognition, and establish an emotional connection with your target audience.* 

### 1. **Understanding Color Theory**

**Primary Colors:** Red, blue, and yellow. These are the foundation of all other colors.

**Secondary Colors:** Green, orange, and purple, formed by mixing primary colors.

**Tertiary Colors:** Created by mixing a primary color with a secondary color (e.g., red-orange, blue-green).

**Color Wheel:** A visual representation of colors arranged according to their chromatic relationship. The color wheel is crucial for understanding color harmony.

### 2. **Color Harmony**

**Complementary Colors:**

- Located opposite each other on the color wheel (e.g., red and green).
- High contrast and vibrant when used together, but can be overwhelming if overused.

**Analogous Colors:**

- Located next to each other on the color wheel (e.g., blue, blue-green, and green).
- Create a harmonious and pleasing look, often found in nature.

**Triadic Colors:**

- Three colors evenly spaced around the color wheel (e.g., red, yellow, blue).
- Offers a balanced and vibrant palette, maintaining contrast while being harmonious.

**Monochromatic Colors:**

- Variations of a single hue with different levels of saturation and brightness.
- Creates a cohesive and soothing look but can lack contrast.

### 3. **Psychology of Colors in Branding**

*Understanding how different colors influence perception and emotions is essential for effective branding.*

**Red:**

- Conveys excitement, passion, and urgency.
- Often used in the food industry and for call-to-action buttons.

**Blue:**

- Represents trust, reliability, and calmness.
- Common in corporate branding (e.g., finance, tech) to instill confidence.

**Yellow:**

- Evokes happiness, optimism, and warmth.
- Effective in grabbing attention and is often used in children's products and leisure brands.

**Green:**

- Associated with nature, health, and tranquility.
- Popular among brands focused on sustainability, health, and wellness.

**Purple:**

- Represents luxury, creativity, and wisdom.
- Used by brands that want to convey a sense of premium quality or innovation.

**Orange:**

- Conveys enthusiasm, creativity, and warmth.
- Often used for brands targeting younger audiences or to promote fun and energy.

**Black:**

- Signifies sophistication, elegance, and power.
- Common in luxury and high-end brands.

**White:**

- Represents purity, simplicity, and cleanliness.
- Used in minimalist branding and by brands that emphasize clarity and openness.

**Gray:**

- Conveys neutrality, professionalism, and balance.
- Often used in technology and automotive brands.

**Brown:**

- Associated with ruggedness, reliability, and warmth.
- Popular in outdoor brands and products that emphasize natural or organic qualities.

### 4. **Applying Color Theory in Branding**

**1. Define Brand Identity and Values:**

- Understand the core values and message your brand wants to convey.
- Choose colors that align with these values and the emotions you want to evoke.

**2. Know Your Audience:**

- Consider the cultural and psychological associations your target audience has with different colors.
- Customize your color palette to resonate with your audience's preferences and expectations.

**3. Create a Color Palette:**

- Use the color wheel and harmony principles to create a balanced and effective palette.
- Consider a primary color (dominant in your brand), secondary colors (complementary), and accent colors (used sparingly for emphasis).

**4. Test Color Combinations:**

- Experiment with different color combinations to see how they look in various contexts (e.g., digital, print, packaging).
- Ensure that the colors work well together and maintain legibility, especially for text and logos.

**5. Consistency Across Platforms:**

- Ensure consistent use of colors across all brand touchpoints, including website, social media, packaging, and advertising.
- Consistency reinforces brand recognition and builds trust with the audience.

**6. Adapt to Trends Without Losing Identity:**

- While it's important to stay relevant, avoid following trends that conflict with your brand’s identity.
- Update your color palette when necessary but keep the core colors consistent to maintain brand recognition.

## Typography

*Typography plays a critical role in branding because fonts communicate personality, evoke emotions, and shape perceptions of your brand. Understanding how to use typography strategically can enhance your brand identity, improve readability, and create a cohesive and memorable experience for your audience.*

### 1. **Types of Typography**

### **a. Serif Fonts**

- **Characteristics**: Traditional, elegant, and timeless, with small lines ("serifs") at the ends of letters.
- **Best For**: Brands aiming for sophistication, authority, or heritage.
- **Examples**: Times New Roman, Georgia, Baskerville.

### **b. Sans Serif Fonts**

- **Characteristics**: Modern, clean, and minimalistic with no extra lines at letter ends.
- **Best For**: Contemporary, approachable, or tech-focused brands.
- **Examples**: Helvetica, Arial, Open Sans.

### **c. Script Fonts**

- **Characteristics**: Decorative, flowing, and handwritten styles.
- **Best For**: Personal, romantic, or artistic brands.
- **Examples**: Brush Script, Lobster, Pacifico.

### **d. Display Fonts**

- **Characteristics**: Bold, dramatic, and unique, often used for headlines.
- **Best For**: Attention-grabbing, edgy, or innovative brands.
- **Examples**: Impact, Bebas Neue, Playfair Display.

### **e. Monospace Fonts**

- **Characteristics**: Each character takes up the same space, creating a mechanical feel.
- **Best For**: Futuristic, tech-oriented, or minimalist brands.
- **Examples**: Courier, Consolas, Space Mono.
