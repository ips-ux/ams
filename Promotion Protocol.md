# Release Preparation

*Complete all steps in this section before moving into the Label Signing System or Self-Release Strategy. This is your foundation, skipping steps here creates problems downstream.*

---

### 1. Register with a PRO (BMI or ASCAP)

*A Performing Rights Organization (PRO) collects royalties on your behalf every time your music is performed, broadcast, or streamed publicly. You must register before your release goes live or you will forfeit those earnings.*

- **Which PRO Should I Choose?**
    - **BMI** and **ASCAP** are the two primary PROs in the United States. Both collect the same types of royalties — the choice largely comes down to personal preference and which labels or collaborators you work with.
    - **BMI**: Free to join. Historically preferred by artists in electronic and hip-hop genres.
    - **ASCAP**: $50 one-time signup fee. Historically preferred by pop and commercial artists.
    - You can only be a member of one PRO at a time.
- **Registration Links**
    - [Join BMI](https://www.bmi.com/creators)
    - [Join ASCAP](https://www.ascap.com/music-creators/join)
    - [SoundExchange](https://www.soundexchange.com/) *(separate registration — collects digital performance royalties from Pandora, SiriusXM, and internet radio)*
- **What to Register**
    - Register every song individually in your PRO account after joining.
    - You will need: Song title, writer splits (if co-written), publisher information (if applicable), and ISRC code.
    - Register songs before they are released — royalties accrue from the date of first use.

---

### 2. Claim Your Artist Profiles

*Claiming verified artist profiles gives you control over how your music is presented on each platform and unlocks analytics, editorial pitch tools, and promotional features.*

- **Priority Platforms to Claim**
    - **Spotify for Artists**: [artists.spotify.com](http://artists.spotify.com) — Required for editorial playlist pitching.
    - **Apple Music for Artists**: [artists.apple.com](http://artists.apple.com) — Required for verified profile and insights.
    - **Amazon Music for Artists**: [artists.amazonmusic.com](http://artists.amazonmusic.com)
    - **YouTube Official Artist Channel**: Contact your distributor or apply via YouTube Help — consolidates auto-generated and uploaded content under one verified channel.
    - **Pandora AMP**: [amp.pandora.com](http://amp.pandora.com) — Allows artist-curated playlists and audio messages.
    - **Deezer for Creators**: [deezerartists.com](http://deezerartists.com)
    - **SoundCloud for Artists**: [artists.soundcloud.com](http://artists.soundcloud.com)
- **What to Update on Each Profile**
    - Branded profile picture (consistent across all platforms)
    - Header or banner image
    - Artist biography
    - Social media links
    - Website link
    - Pin your most recent or most important release

---

### 3. Prepare All Final Mixes

*Before anything is distributed or sent to a label, every mix version must be finalized, named correctly, and exported at the proper specifications.*

- **Music Assets**
    - **Radio Mix**
        - *Shortened mix optimized for streaming services. Typically 3:00–4:00 in length.*
    - **Extended Mix**
        - *Full-length mix optimized for DJ playability. Typically 5:00–7:00 in length.*
    - **Instrumental Mix**
        - *Mix with all vocals removed. Used for sync licensing placements.*
    - **Acapella Mix**
        - *Mix containing only vocals and no music. Used for remix opportunities.*
    - **Full Stems**
        - *Individual exported stems (drums, bass, synths, vocals, FX, etc.). Used for mastering and remix opportunities.*
- **Export Specifications**
    - **Format**: WAV (preferred) or AIFF
    - **Bit Depth**: 24-bit
    - **Sample Rate**: 44.1 kHz (or 48 kHz if required by label)
    - **Loudness**: Master to -1 dBTP true peak. Reference target LUFS against platform standards (Spotify: -14 LUFS, Apple Music: -16 LUFS integrated)
    - **File Naming Convention**: `Artist Name - Song Title (Mix Type)` — e.g., `MASTERIA - Escape Reality (Extended Mix)`

---

### 4. Set Up Your Folder Structure

*Organize every asset for the release in a single, clearly labeled folder before distribution or promotion begins. Disorganization at this stage creates delays and errors downstream.*

- **Recommended Folder Structure**
    
    ```
    Release Name/
    ├── Audio/
    │   ├── Radio Mix
    │   ├── Extended Mix
    │   ├── Instrumental Mix
    │   ├── Acapella Mix
    │   └── Stems/
    ├── Artwork/
    │   ├── Cover Art (3000x3000)
    │   ├── Header Images/
    │   └── Promotional Graphics/
    ├── Video/
    │   ├── Visualizer/
    │   └── Short-Form Content/
    ├── Press/
    │   ├── Press Release
    │   └── EPK
    └── Distribution/
        ├── ISRC Codes
        └── Metadata Sheet
    ```
    

---

### 5. Add Metadata

*Metadata is embedded information inside your audio files that tells streaming platforms, distributors, and PROs who owns the song and how to credit it. Missing or incorrect metadata results in lost royalties and misattributed plays.*

- **Required Metadata Fields**
    - **Track Title**: Use your official release title exactly as it will appear on streaming platforms.
    - **Artist Name**: Your official artist name, spelled and capitalized consistently.
    - **Album / EP / Single Title**: If part of a larger body of work.
    - **Genre**: Primary genre classification.
    - **Release Date**: Confirm this matches your distribution date.
    - **ISRC Code**: International Standard Recording Code — unique identifier for each recording. Your distributor assigns this if you don't have one.
    - **UPC/EAN Code**: Assigned by your distributor for the full release.
    - **BPM**: Embed the track's tempo for DJ platforms and sync searches.
    - **Key**: Embed the musical key for mix compatibility.
    - **Copyright Line**: `℗ [Year] [Rights Holder Name]`
    - **Publishing Line**: `© [Year] [Publisher or Artist Name]`
    - **Composer / Songwriter Credits**: Full legal names of all writers.
    - **Producer Credit**: Your name or production alias.
    - **Label Name**: Your label or self-release imprint name.
- **Recommended Metadata Tools**
    - [Mp3tag](https://www.mp3tag.de/en/) *(free, Windows/Mac — the industry standard for batch metadata editing)*
    - [Kid3](https://kid3.kde.org/) *(free, cross-platform alternative)*

# Label Signing System

*Use this system to research, pitch, and track label submissions for every record you want to sign. Work your list consistently — most placements come from persistence, not perfection.*

---

### 1. Target Labels

Prioritize target labels to sign with. Use the “Labels” spreadsheet in your AMS to do this.

**Target Criteria**

- **Audience Size**: Prioritize labels with the greatest potential reach on social and streaming platforms.
- **Notoriety**: Target labels with demonstrated notoriety or clout in the music industry, as association with them will help your reputation.
- **Live Events**: Find labels who have live events with the artists on the labels, as this will open doors to live events for you to perform at.
- **Label Owner**: Prioritize labels who’s owner is an artist you look up to, or would like to be associated with in the future.

### 2. Contact Info

Identify submission contact information for all your top labels. Keep track of this information in your “Labels” spreadsheet.

**Primary Identification Process**

1. Search Google and LinkedIn for their A&R
    - “*[label name] A&R*” or “[label name] manager”
2. List their name + contact information in AMS
3. Follow that A&R on all social media channels

**Backup Identification Process**

- Ask label artists for their label submission info
- Ask any label employee for demo contact info
- Send music to demo email on website
- Use tools like LabelRadar to send your music

### 3. Send Music

Submit your music to your top labels, starting with top priority labels first. Track submissions in your “Labels” spreadsheet.

**How to Send Music**

1. Properly label your music (*Artist Name - Song (Mix Type)).*
2. Ensure file metadata is updated.
3. Create a private Soundcloud playlist with 2-4 songs.
    1. *Enable downloads*
    2. *Hide stats*
4. Prioritize labels, sending to up to 5 at a time.
5. Send email using Email Templates.
6. Track sending results in AMS.

**Email Templates**

- **Initial Email**
    
    *Use this template as your initial email to label A&R’s.*
    
    Hi [A&R NAME],
    
    Hope you're having a great week. I'm an artist from [HOMETOWN], with releases on [LABELS] and recent support by [ARTISTS].
    
    Been a huge fan of [LABEL NAME] for years, and just wrapped up a few demo's I think you might like for your label:
    
    [SOUNDCLOUD LINK]
    
    Would love to hear any thoughts you have on these!
    
    Thank you,
    
    [YOUR NAME]
    
- **Follow-Up Email**
    
    *Use this template as a follow-up email to label A&R’s.*
    
    Hi [A&R NAME],
    
    Just checking back in here to see if you have any interest in these ones?
    
    Thanks in advance,
    
    [YOUR NAME]
    

### 4. Follow-Up & Repeat

*Use steps below to follow-up with labels who you submitted to. Continue down your list of priority labels as you receive responses.*

- Follow-up after 2 weeks if you don’t hear back
    - Adjust your follow-up based on email opens and listen activity
    - Use our Follow-Up Templates
- At this point, begin sending your next batch of email to the next target labels on your list
- Track all results in your AMS

# Promotion Strategies

*Apply these strategies to every release, regardless of whether it’s on a label or self-released. Promotion is not optional — the quality of your music and the quality of your campaign are equally important.*

---

### Pre-Promotion Planning

*We must first identify a cohesive theme, narrative, and meaning for your song. This will allow us to tell a story around your release, which will be the foundation of promotion we use.*

- What meaning does your song have?
- How could the song teach fans about your brand?
- How does the song title relate to your brand?
- How do the lyrics relate to your brand?
- How could your song relate to listeners?

### Social

*Creating awareness for your release using content on social media.*

**1. Plan Content**

*Strategize the pieces of content you will create.*

- Identify themes and meanings in your song you could attach content to
- Reference your AMS for content ideas you could relate to those themes
- Brainstorm a list of at least 10 content ideas
- Prioritize those ideas based on potential virality

**2. Create Content**

*Record and design the content you planned in previous step.*

- Record at least 5-6 pieces of content relating to your release.
- Edit or outsource the final editing of your content.
- Prepare all relevant captions and hashtags ahead of time.

**3. Schedule Content**

*Develop schedule to roll out each individual piece of content.*

- Add planned content to your social calendar.
- Centralize all assets, captions, and hashtags in your AMS.
- Ensure your available to engage with content at scheduled times.

**4. Share Content**

*Reveal and promote content on social platforms.*

- Post content across all applicable platforms
- Share all posts to your story and community for additional engagement.
- Provide re-engagement and gratitude for all followers who engage with you.

### Streaming

*Maximizing traction on streaming platforms to build traction for your release.*

**The Four Platform Growth Factors**

*Every streaming platform rewards the same four behaviors. Build your strategy around these consistently.*

- **Content**: High-quality audio and visual content optimized for each specific platform.
- **Consistency**: Regular releases and uploads signal to algorithms that your profile is active.
- **Engagement**: Streams, likes, saves, follows, and comments all improve your algorithmic ranking.
- **Playlisting**: Getting placed on playlists — editorial, curator, and algorithmic — dramatically amplifies reach.

# Performance Strategies

*Booking live shows is a long game that rewards consistency and relationship-building. Use the outreach system below to build genuine connections with promoters before you ever need something from them.*

---

### Outreach Strategy

**Step 1: Identify Key Promoters in Your Area**

*Build a list of relevant promoters and brands that book shows in your genre and region.*

- Go through your past shows/concerts and write down who hosted or promoted them.
- Search local venues’ Instagram/Facebook event pages and note repeat event organizers.
- Create a Notion database with the following columns:
    - Promoter Name
    - Company Name
    - Instagram
    - Website
    - Venue(s) Used
    - Genre Fit (1–5)
    - Notes

> Tip: Focus on promoters already booking artists with a similar sound or brand.
> 

---

**Step 2: Research Key Contacts & Connect**

*Warm up the relationship before reaching out cold.*

1. **Look for individuals behind the company:**
    - Use LinkedIn, Instagram, and the company’s About page to identify:
        - Talent buyers
        - Event coordinators
        - Founders or booking agents
2. **Follow the company + key employees on Instagram.**
3. **Begin engaging** (for at least 1–2 weeks):
    - Like recent posts
    - Leave thoughtful comments (no spammy emojis)
    - Respond to stories if appropriate (“That lineup is insane” / “This show looks massive”)

> Why: You want them to recognize your name before they see your email.
> 

---

**Step 3: Watch for Opportunities**

*Wait for shows that align with your sound.*

- Monitor their IG and email lists for new show announcements
- Look for artists whose energy/sound/brand matches yours
- Prioritize shows where you're already a fan or know the crowd would love you

---

**Step 4: Reach Out (Send Email)**

*Send a high-quality, custom email when the time is right.*

Use this format:

---

**Subject:** Potential Support for [Artist Name] on [Show Name / Date]

---

Hi there,

Hope you're doing well. I’ve been a huge fan of your shows for years, ever since I first went to [your first show with them]. Noticed you just announced [Show Name] with [Headliner] and thought I’d reach out.

My name is [Your Name], and I’m a [genre] producer/DJ based in [City]. I’ve been building momentum with recent releases on [Label Name(s)] and performances at [mention 1–2 notable shows/festivals]. 

Not sure if you have support figured out for [Show Name], but I’d love the chance to be considered for a support slot for this event or any future opportunities that you feel could potentially be a fit.

If helpful, here’s a quick snapshot:

- EPK: [Link]
- Music: [Link Spotify/ SoundCloud]
- Live Recap/Performance Footage: [Link]

Thanks for all you’re doing for the scene. I’d be honored to contribute to one of your lineups.

Best,

[Your Full Name]

[Phone] | [IG] | [Website]

---

**Step 5: Follow Up**

*Stay top of mind without being annoying.*

- If no response to your first email, follow up every couple months with a new email and variations to the template above, ideally targeting different employees within the company.
- Continue engaging on social.
