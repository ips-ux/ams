# Spark Strategies

### ✅ How to Use:

1. Select the type of **Instrument** you would like to begin with.
2. Experiment with **Sound Design Strategies** below for that instrument to find inspiration.
3. If you are struggling developing a Spark, experiment with **Creative Challenges** or **Creative Mental Models**.

---

## **Drum Strategies**

- **Drum Design**: Begin building and layering a kick drum, snare, or clap.
- **Groove Design**: Build a foundational drum groove to expand upon.
- **Kick Groove**: Create a drum groove with only kicks or other low end elements.
- **Loop Inspiration**: Find a drum loop which inspires you and expand upon it.
- **Groove Recreation**: Rebuild the drop drum beat of a favorite reference track.
- **Drum Build**: Create an interesting drum build for your song.
- **Intro Progression**: Start by creating a drum progression for your intro.
- **Fill Collection**: Collect a selection of fills to use throughout your drop, then build your drum groove around them.
- **Loop Manipulation**: Warp, reverse, or manipulate drum loop to find interesting patterns.
- **Loop Rearrange**: Rearrange an existing drum loop which inspires you.
- **Build Recreation**: Recreate a drum build from a favorite reference track.
- **MIDI Experimentation**: Create interesting drum MIDI pattern, then experiment with placing different sounds into your drum rack.
- **Sample Collection**: Create a collection of compelling drum sounds, then build a loop with those sounds.
- **Fill Creation**: Build interesting drum fills to use throughout your drop.
- **Drum Design**: Using a drum designer plugin like Kick2 to build drum elements from scratch.
- **Offbeat Groove**: Begin with toms or other percussive sounds to create a unique, offbeat pattern and groove for your drums.
- **Top Loop Creation**: Create a groovy, compelling top loop using only hi-hats and percussion.
- **Filtered Minimal**: Build a minimal drum loop using filtered sounds for your intro or break.
- **AI Loop Creation**: Use an AI music generator like Suno or Soundfull to generate an interesting drum loop to expand upon.

## **Sound Design Strategies**

- **Evolving Synth**: Create a well-automated evolving synth for your build or break.
- **Preset Inspiration**: Find a preset which inspires you, and begin arranging different notes into a melody.
- **Sound Recreation**: Recreate the bass or synths from a favorite reference track.
- **Synth Design**: Experiment with designing an interesting synth or bass sound until inspired.
- **Loop Manipulation**: Try warping, reversing, chopping, or processing an interesting loop.
- **Loop Randomization**: Put a bass or synth loop into a randomizer to find interesting sounds or patterns.
- **Automation Recording**: Record yourself experimenting with presets and extensive processing chains, then isolate audio to start your song with.
- **LFO Manipulation**: Experiment with an LFO plugin on a sustained sound until you find a pattern you find interesting.
- **Sample Combination**: Combine different loops and samples into a compelling melodic phrase or pattern.
- **Lead Layering**: Begin layering different sounds together to create a compelling lead sound.
- **Rhythm Automation**: Create an automated rhythm you can use as a lead sound during your build or drop.
- **Experimental FX**: Use an experimental FX plugin like Output Portal to create interesting tones and textures to resample.
- **Cinematic Sound Design**: Build a rumbling, cinematic sub pattern for your break.
- **Sub Processing**: Create a bass pattern using just a sine wave, then begin heavily processing that pattern to bring out the midrange and high end.
- **One Shot Randomization**: Load a series of one shots into a drum machine, then use a randomizer to create interesting patterns and combinations.
- **Sample Manipulation**: Experiment with warping, pitching, and heavily processing an interesting sample.
- **AI Sampling**: Use AI music generator to create samples or stems you can use.
- **Set Intro**: Build a song you can picture using as an opener to your festival live set.

## **Melodic Strategies**

- **Chord to Melody**: Create chord progression using piano, experiment with different presets, then build melody based on that structure.
- **Melody Creation**: Create an interesting melody in MIDI using init synth preset, then scan new presets to use.
- **Layered Loop**: Find an inspiring melodic loop, then begin layering that melody with new sounds.
- **MIDI Rearrange**: Rearranging an existing MIDI file using a preset you like.
- **Preset Exploration**: Drop inspiring loop into MIDI channel, rearrange, and apply new presets.
- **Loop to MIDI**: Find an inspiring audio loop, then transform that loop to MIDI and begin adjusting notes.
- **Classical MIDI**: Download MIDI of classical songs, and begin arranging MIDI notes into new pattern.
- **MIDI Search**: Search for “[song name] MIDI” on Google, download, and rearrange those notes into new pattern.
- **Blank Arrangement**: Create a skeleton arrangement of blank MIDI files for each instrument, based off a reference track
- **AI MIDI**: Create MIDI pattern using an AI MIDI plugin like MIDI Wizard.
- **Plugin MIDI**: Use a Scale, Chords, or Arp plugin to generate interesting MIDI to use.
- **MIDI Jam**: Record yourself playing a series of live rhythms on a MIDI controller, then arrange and clean up that MIDI.
- **Melody Recreation**: Recreate the melody of a reference track, then begin rearranging notes and apply new presets.
- **Orchestral Layering**: Begin layering string and orchestral instruments to create a musical foundation for your break.
- **Bass First**: Create an interesting bassline first, then experiment with layering a complementary lead pattern on top.
- **Bass Melody**: Build a melody you find interesting, then experiment with a bass groove with complements it below.
- **Arp Builder**: Create an arpeggiated pattern to use during your break and build.
- **Reverse Start**: Start with a reversed sample to build an interesting melody.
- **LFO Design**: Synthesize or sample a sustained sound, and use a filter LFO to create an engaging cadence.
- **Manual Arp**: Manually create an arpeggiated sequence using short envelope sounds.
- **Sound Discovery**: Use a sample generation tool like Output Arcade to find inspiring sounds and textures.
- **AI Melody Maker**: Utilize an AI sample generation tool like Suno to create a melody or chord progression to build upon.

## **Vocal Strategies**

- **Vocal to MIDI**: Drag your vocal sample into MIDI, then use this MIDI to build your supporting melody.
- **Acapella Presets**: Drop your acapella in a MIDI channel, then audition different presets to layer onto that acapella.
- **Vocal Chop Lead**: Find a compelling MIDI clip, then experiment with vocal chops you can use as a lead sound.
- **Vocal Foundation**: Use AI vocal generator to create interesting vocal loops or phrases.
- **Vocal Recording**: Record your own vocals, mix and process them, then begin building your song around them.
- **AI Vocals**: Record your own vocals, upload those to an AI voice cloning platform, then download to use in your song.
- **Text to Voice**: Develop compelling lyrics for your song, then use a text-to-voice generator to create your vocals.
- **Vocal Randomizer**: Feed your vocal into a randomizer to develop interesting sounds and rhythms.
- **Vocal Arpeggiator**: Put your vocal loop into an arpeggiator to find interesting patterns.
- **Vocal Beat**: Find an inspiring vocal loop to build a drum beat around.
- **Vocal Chop Phrase**: Chop parts of a vocal loop into a new, creative phrase or melody.
- **Vocal Bassline**: Use the melody or phrasing of your vocal as a structure to build your bassline around.
- **Vocal Distortion**: Add heavy distortion or processing to an interesting vocal to develop new sounds and tones.
- **Vocal Reference Layering**: Reference a track with similar style vocal, and begin compiling similar vocal layers.
- **Vocal Splicing**: Isolate words and phrases of a vocal loop, then splice them into a new vocal arrangement.
- **Vocal Drum Beat**: Find a vocal, then use that cadence or phrasing as a structure to build a compelling drum beat around.
- **Chop Rearrange**: Find a vocal chop loop, then rearrange those chops into a new interesting melody.
- **Vocal Textures**: Create textures and atmospheres using resamples from vocal reverbs or delays with heavy processing.
- **Movie Vocals**: Resample the vocals for an movie or tv show which could be used in your track.
- **Call and Response**: Create an exciting call-and-response vocal chant loop to use in your drop.
- **Pre-Drop Isolation**: Place a pre-drop vocal in your mix, then isolate words from that vocal to use across your drop.
- **Rhythmic Vocal Chops**: Create a rhythmic pattern using chopped pieces of an interesting vocal phrase or loop.
- **Vocal Isolation**: Use vocal isolator to fully isolate vocals for a favorite song, then apply acapella techniques above to create remix.
- **Acapella to MIDI**: Download the acapella from a song online, drag that audio into MIDI, then build a melody around that remix acapella.
- **Chord Progression**: Download the acapella from a song online, and build an evolving chord progression to support that vocal.
- **Vocal Bassline**: Use the acapella from a song as a foundation to build a new bassline around.
- **Vocal Plugins**: Use a vocal sample plugin like Output Exhale to create interesting melodic idea.
- **Vocal Arp Loop**: Find a vocal arp loop to begin layering with additional sounds.
- **Rhythmic Breathing**: Utilize breathing or mouth noise samples as a rhythmic foundation for your track.
- **Choir Design**: Use a choir to build a powerful, orchestral section of your song.
- **Bass Vocal Layers**: Create layers of your bassline by using vocals pitched down significantly.
- **Speech Sample**: Find an interesting section of a speech or interview online, then use that as vocals in your song.
- **Percussive Processing**: Add significant processing (echos, distortion, ect) to percussive vocal sounds, then arrange into a loop.
- **LFO Chopping**: Use a filter or volume LFO to a vocal loop to begin chopping into new melodic cadence.
- **Spatial Fills**: Remove words from a vocal loop, and use dry/wet automations on delay to fill those gaps with new interesting vocal content.

## **FX Strategies**

- **Automated Riser**: Build a riser for your track in your favorite VST with interesting automations.
- **Resample Textures**: Resample interesting sounds to create foundational textures and atmospheres for your track.
- **Organic Vibes**: Identify compelling organic sounds and recordings to create a vibe for your track.
- **Break Theme**: Build a foundational theme and vibe for the break of your track using FX.
- **Automation Tension**: Create tension with interesting automations on a sound over 16 or 32 bars.
- **FX Melody**: Use FX shots as primary sound to build an interesting melody.
- **Call-and-Response FX**: Create an energetic call-and-response pattern with FX for your track.
- **LFO Inspiration**: Add an LFO to sustained FX sounds to find inspiration.
- **FX Drop Layer**: Create an exciting foundational layer for your drop using FX, noise, lasers, ect.
- **Theme Folder**: Identify a theme for your track, and create a folder of FX sounds which match that theme.
- **Reverb Swells**: Use reverb swells to create a spacious and atmospheric intro or build.
- **FX Arrangement**: Build an arrangement for your break and build using impactful FX hits.
- **Stutter Foundation**: Add a stutter effect to sustained sounds to create a glitchy, rhythmic foundation.
- **Alarm Foundation**: Begin with an alarm or exciting FX sounds to create an interesting foundation or build.
- **Crowd Energy**: Assemble and arrange crowd sounds in an interesting pattern to create an energetic foundation.
- **Environment FX**: Identify an environment for your song, and build a folder of FX that exist within that environment (i.e. a song about Detroit would use mechanical sounds, ect).
- **Warped FX**: Experiment with warping, pitching, and reversing FX shots or loops to identify interesting textures or hits.
- **Rising FX**: Creating an automated, rising FX sound for your build.

## Creative Challenges

- **Time Restriction:** Setting time limits for creating your idea.
    1. Set a timer for a limited period of time, ideally between 5-30 minutes.
    2. Create as quickly as possible, building as much as you can during this time period.
    3. Stop once your timer goes off, export the track to your AMS, and repeat the process. 
- **Sound Restriction:** Limiting the samples available to you.
    1. Build a small collection of 5-10 samples.
    2. Create your entire idea using this limited selection of sounds.
    3. If you get stuck, think about how you can more creatively manipulate or resample the sounds in your collection.
    4. Once your idea is complete, export the track to your AMS, and repeat the process. 
- **Plugin Restriction:** Use just a single VST plugin to create your idea.
    1. Identify a VST instrument you’d like to build your idea with.
    2. Create all sounds in your idea using just this VST.
    3. Once your idea is complete, export the track to your AMS, and repeat the process. 
- **Genre Swap:** Create an idea in a genre you usually don’t produce in.
    1. Identify a new genre you’d like to try creating.
    2. Add a couple reference tracks in this genre you can use as inspiration.
    3. Begin building your idea. Your drums and groove can be a great place to start.
- **Track Hacking:** Recreate a reference track which inspires you.
    1. Identify a reference track which you’d like to recreate.
    2. Listen carefully to identify all layering going on in the drop, creating a unique channel for each.
    3. Begin recreating the drum groove first, followed by the bassline, then melodic elements.
    4. Feel free to adjust the bassline or melody to make the track your own once you get to a good point.
- **Set Intro**: Create the type of song you would open a festival set with.
    1. Picture yourself opening a live set in the future - the crowd, visuals, stage, ect.
    2. Begin collecting sounds and textures that would fit this set.
    3. Arrange these sounds into the intro or build of a song.
- **Silent Production:** Create your idea in silence with your speakers turned off.
    1. Identify a collection of sounds or presets you would like to use for this challenge.
    2. Turn off your speakers. You will rely only on your eyes to create your idea.
    3. Begin arranging your sounds and creating your melodic phrasing.
    4. Create your full 8-bar loop before you listen for the first time.
    5. Listen for any necessary adjustments before exporting your idea to your AMS.
- **Time Capsule**: Produce a song that would be popular in 50 years.
    1. Imagine what music will sound like in 50 years from now.
    2. Create a collection of sounds that would fit this era of music.
    3. Begin arranging an idea using these futuristic sounds, with the idea of a release in 50 years.
    4. Once complete, export your project to your AMS.
- **Remix**: Create a remix of a popular song or acapella.
    1. Identify a song or acapella which inspires you.
    2. Download this song and add it to your DAW.
    3. Begin creating your idea, using primary elements from the original as inspiration.
    4. Once the main theme of your remix is complete, export your idea to your AMS.
- **No Quantization**: Produce without quantization for a more human/organic feel.
    1. Turn off the grid in your DAW.
    2. Utilize Song Spark Strategies above to begin creating your idea.
    3. Use your ears and eyes to time elements in your song and create your groove.
    4. Once your idea is complete, export your idea to your AMS.
- **One Sample**: Create a track using only one sample.
    1. Identify a simple sample you will use for your whole idea.
    2. Begin warping, pitching, processing, and manipulating this sample to create different sounds.
    3. Use these new sound variations to begin creating your idea.
    4. Focus on building as much as possible here. It won’t be easy, but will help you think more creatively about your sounds.
    5. Once complete, export this idea to your AMS.
- **Storytelling**: Produce an idea which tells a story.
    1. Identify a story you’d like to tell with your idea.
    2. Begin creating a melody or collecting sounds that align with this story.
    3. Focus on building your idea around the most interesting part of the story first.
    4. Once complete, export this idea to your AMS.

## Creative Mental Models

- **The Ocean Explorer:** Seeking uncharted territory.
    1. Start with a basic rhythm, melody or sound.
    2. Allow your intuition to guide the development of the song, much like how the wind guides a sailboat.
    3. Stay open to unexpected musical ideas that may lead you to uncharted territories; don’t be afraid to change course if needed.
- **The Inventor:** Experimenting with technology.
    1. Use plugins, racks, AI or other devices that assist in the generation of melodies, rhythms, sounds or other musical ideas.
    2. Utilize randomization: develop and experiment with randomized outputs.
    3. Tinker with unusual plugin chains until unique results emerge.
- **The Chef:** Developing a secret recipe.
    1. Identify the key ingredients of your song (melody, rhythm, harmony).
    2. Like a chef, experiment with combining these elements in different ways.
    3. Refine the mix until you have a musical recipe that tastes just right.
- **The Visual Artist:** Painting a sonic picture.
    1. Start with a sound that evokes a strong image or scene in your mind.
    2. Develop the song around this visual, using the scene to guide your choice of musical elements.
    3. Refine the song until it vividly brings the initial image or scene to life.
- **The Novelist:** Writing about a theme or subject.
    1. Come up with a title or central concept for your song.
    2. Imagine what sounds, rhythms, and melodies could convey this concept.
    3. Develop these elements into a song that tells the story of your initial concept.
- **The Architect:** Develop a blueprint.
    1. Like an architect, plan out the structure of your song in detail before you begin.
    2. Carefully construct each part of the song, making sure each element serves a purpose.
    3. Revise and refine the song until every part is in its right place, creating a harmonious structure.
- **The Gardener:** Plant a seed and let nature decide.
    1. Plant the seed of a basic melody or rhythm.
    2. Nurture this idea, allowing it to grow and develop organically.
    3. Continue to refine and prune the song as it develops, until it has grown into a mature composition.
